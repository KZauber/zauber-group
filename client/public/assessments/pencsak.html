What We Found

01 Directory & Listings
C
Pencsak is out there. You're on Houzz, the BBB, and a handful of other directories. Your BBB rating is A+, and you've earned recognition from the Greater San Antonio Builders Association. That's all real and it matters.

The problem: when someone searches for a custom home builder on Google Maps, your star rating doesn't appear. Your Google Business Profile either isn't fully optimized or doesn't have enough reviews to trigger a star display. That's the first thing a prospective client sees. Right now it's blank. Yelp, Angi, and Apple Maps presence could not be confirmed at all.

You have a presence but it's fragmented. The directories that matter most aren't working hard enough for you yet.
02 AI & Google Search
D
High-income buyers increasingly start their search by asking ChatGPT or Perplexity: "Who are the best luxury home builders in San Antonio?" We searched those exact phrases. Pencsak Builders did not appear anywhere.

This connects directly to the traffic data above. Your website gets 43 organic visitors a month. All of your 1,600-search-per-month keywords are stuck on pages 3 through 6. AI tools pull their recommendations from sites with strong Google rankings and review volume — and right now, you don't have either.

Your blog has also been inactive since July 2023 — nearly three years with no new content. Google treats that as a freshness signal. Competitors who publish regularly rank higher.
03 Website
C
The site looks professional and the photography is excellent — which makes sense given Angela's background. The phone number is in the header. The contact form qualifies prospects by budget and lot status. These are genuinely strong.

But three structural issues are keeping the site invisible. First: not a single page has a meta description — the line of text Google shows under your link in search results. Google is writing its own, and it's not doing a great job. Second: the homepage headline is "Welcome Home!" — that's a tagline, not a keyword. Third: you name nine cities you serve but have no dedicated page for any of them. A buyer in New Braunfels searching "custom home builder New Braunfels" will never find you — there's simply nothing for Google to show them.

These aren't redesign problems. They're fixable without touching how the site looks.
04 Social Media
C
Instagram is your strongest channel. 3,867 followers and 324 posts shows consistency, and the content quality is above what most builders in this market produce. Angela's photography background shows.

Facebook is the gap. 852 page likes and zero reviews. The 40-to-60-year-old homeowner at Pencsak's price point spends more time on Facebook than anywhere else online. It's also the platform where local trust is built through reviews and shared recommendations. Your Facebook page doesn't reflect the quality of the business.

YouTube is linked from the site but activity is unknown. Video walkthroughs of finished projects are among the highest-converting content a luxury builder can have.
05 Paid Advertising
F
We checked Facebook's public ad library. Pencsak Builders has no active ads — not now, not recently. No Google Ads either.

Your competitors are running paid campaigns right now — targeting homeowners in Boerne, New Braunfels, Stone Oak, and Vintage Oaks who are actively researching builders. Pencsak isn't appearing in those feeds.

At your price point — $1.2 million and up — a single new client from a well-run Meta campaign pays for an entire year of advertising many times over. No other marketing channel offers that kind of return on a per-lead basis.
06 Lead Follow-Up
D
Your contact form is better than most — you're already asking about budget, land ownership, and whether they have plans. That's smart pre-qualification. But once someone submits, there's no confirmed automated response. No instant text, no email confirmation, no acknowledgment that their form was received.

You use BuilderTrend for active project management, which is excellent. But BuilderTrend doesn't handle pre-sale leads. The gap is the window between "form submitted" and "you see it and respond" — which could be hours or days. In that window, your prospect is also calling the next builder on their list.

The builder who responds first with substance wins the most attention. At the $1.2M+ level, buyers are making a shortlist — not an impulse decision.
07 Reviews & Reputation
D
This is the single most significant gap — especially given how long Pencsak has been building and how strong the work is.

Across every public platform: 2 confirmed reviews — both on Houzz, both 5-star. No confirmed Google reviews. Zero on Facebook, BuildZoom, BBB, or Angi. The website has four client testimonials but those are controlled by you — they carry less weight with a buyer who's trying to verify your reputation independently.

The builders appearing in AI search results and Google Maps recommendations have 20, 50, even 100+ Google reviews. That volume is a ranking signal. You have 12 industry awards from the Greater San Antonio Builders Association — Best Kitchen, Best Master Suite, Judges' Choice, Peoples' Choice. You've been winning since 2021. None of that appears when someone Googles your name and looks for social proof from real clients.
